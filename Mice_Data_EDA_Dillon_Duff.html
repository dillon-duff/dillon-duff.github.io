Discuss¶

In this context, dimensionality reduction allows us to reduce the volume of features we are looking at in our dataset and it allows us to reduce the noise of certain chemicals or columns that have little effect.

With less dimensions, we are also more easily able to visualize the data and view possible clusters. With higher dimensionality, this may not be possible.

Discussion¶

PCA and LDA are similar in some ways, but one of the largest differences is that PCA in unsupervised while LDA is supervised. That is why the results may be more interesting for LDA, since we can actually see how well classes are separated.

PCA, though, is used as a technique to preprocess data and allow us to employ stronger methods of classification since the dimensionality can be reduced. For this dataset in particular, PCA is useful because it can take ~80 features and convert them into 10 features while maintaining 80% of the explained variance. With less features, computation becomes less expensive and this can allow you to try more methods. PCA also can show possible clusters or outliers in data, so it is nice for exploratory analysis.

Similarly, LDA can be use as a preprocessing technique to enable the use of other classification methods. LDA is supervised, so it requires having labelled data, but it is very efficient at maximizing separability between classes and can enable very easy classification.
